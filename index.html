ENERGY IN AUSTRALIA

An overview of Australia's Energy Export, Generation, Consumption and Future

Global Net Energy Exports in 2020

In 2020, Australia had the 3rd largest net export in energy (exports minus imports) of 13,200 Petajoules, after Russia (28,800 PJ) and Saudi Arabia (16,200 PJ).

Main energy sources include coal and natural gas, exported primarily to China, Japan, South Korea and India. Australia's net export equates to over 2/3 of primary energy production.

Australia's Generated Energy over the Years

Rich in non-renewable energy resources, Australia has vast reserves throughout the country.

Federal and state policies have been favouring the reduction of coal usage and promoting natural gas, a cleaner alternative, producing fewer carbon emissions when burned for electricity generation.

Primary Energy Consumption Across States and Territories, 10 Years Apart

The Northern Territory and Western Australia have their mining and manufacturing sectors account for 53.5% and 37.5% of electricity generation respectively (while no more than 10% for other states). This leads to NT and WA having larger consumption per capita, as a lower proportion of energy consumption comes from the electricity sector.

Australia's Future in Renewable Energy

In the 2000s, wind energy grew rapidly from technological advances and decrease in wind turbine costs. The 2010s marked the acceleration in large-scale solar farms, driven by the Renewable Energy Target (RET). Fast forward to today, compared to the 1990s, renewable energy contributes four-times as much in Australia's energy mix, facilitating the country's efforts in reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
